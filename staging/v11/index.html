About us

How They Met

Kalah and Brent originally met while working at Arnold Worldwide in Boston. However, they did not speak much until Brent left Arnold. After their first real introduction at a mutual friend’s house, they continued to run into each other at various events. The more they saw each other the more the friendship grew.

After numerous hours of chatting about life, music, aspirations and morals, they saw each other more frequently, and the group of friends got smaller and smaller. On crutches, Brent accompanied Kalah to Countryfest with a few of her Bentley friends. Brent returned the favor by taking Kalah to opening weekend of Bills vs Patriots with some of his friends from Arnold; if you know Brent you that is a BIG DEAL.

Our First Date

In the midst of all this, Brent accepted a job to return to Arnold Worldwide. Even though this changed the dynamics a bit, Brent still went for it and asked Kalah to join him for an advance showing of “Where the Wild Things Are,” where they both sat awkwardly for two hours, trying to figure out, “Is this a date?”

Falling in Love

After that date, (was it a date?), Kalah and Brent soon became inseparable. Together they’ve enjoyed snowboarding, trying new foods, playing all sorts of board games, exploring the great outdoors and experiencing new facets of life. Enter San Francisco. At the end of 2010 they both realized they had an urge to try something new and different in a big way. After careful research and planning, Kalah and Brent decided they would move to San Francisco. They’ve loved every minute of exploring the new city, making friends, shredding Tahoe, becoming wine connoisseurs and enjoying all the beautiful nature around the Bay Area.

The Proposal

For their third year anniversary Kalah and Brent headed to California’s wine region. Brent tried to play it as cool as he could. He took Kalah to a 5-star breakfast that morning; Kalah made funny jokes and created awkward moments. Brent shook his head in disbelief and laughed uncomfortably.

Soon after breakfast, Brent presented Kalah with a wrapped present. Opening it up, she realized it was a scrapbook that Brent had given her for their anniversary the year before, with the instructions to create a book of memories from all of their “dating years.” Thinking that Brent had added to the scrapbook, Kalah proceeded to flip through it, ooo-ing and ahh-ing over memories, as Brent fumbled to get the ring ready. When she got to the end, there was nothing new added. Kalah looked up and said “I don't get it.” As she did, Brent swung his hand around and said “Do you get it now, will you marry me?” After a huge smile and a few tears Kalah said yes, marking the end of their “dating years.”
